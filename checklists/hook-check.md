# Hook Check

Before delivering any draft, slide 1 must pass this data-driven check.

The rules are derived from JSup's own winning posts (100k+ views) versus weaker ones (<60k). They are not guesses.

---

## Lessons from JSup's posts (cold memory integrated)

### What works
- "불편한 소식" style targeting when the reader is directly implicated
- big number with missing reason
- unexpected decision with motive withheld
- one surprising fact + one missing answer + one affected reader

### What fails
- slide 1 containing what + how + consequence together
- corporate-news-headline phrasing
- hype words doing the work instead of the fact

### Style DNA
- Korean, 반말, clipped lines, dry delivery
- strong fact-backed conviction
- universal stakes over insider trivia
- unresolved close more often than neat resolution

---

## S-tier pattern (required for ceiling > 80)

Slide 1 must contain, in the first ~6 lines, AT LEAST:

- **2 numbers** (concrete, no rounding to "많이" or "수많은")
- **2 proper nouns** (company / person / place)
- **1 time-specific marker** (어제 / 3월 26일 / 12개월 / 200년 / 48시간)
- **1 extreme state word** (역사상 가장 / 시세의 10배 / 처음으로 / 같은 날 / 전부)
- **1 of these two**: direct reader targeting ("~한테", "~쓰는 사람") OR an overwhelming scale number that targets nobody but forces attention

Slide 1 should NOT try to be poetic or cinematic. Density beats rhythm.

## Proven opening formulas (from JSup's own wins)

### Formula A — Direct Target + Pattern
Template: `{X}한 사람한테 불편한 소식. {시점1}. {시점2}. {시점3}. {회사1/2/3}가 {구체 동작}.`

Source: 306k view post (AI 무료강의).
```
AI 강의에 수십만원 쓴 사람한테 불편한 소식.
12월 OpenAI. 2월 Google. 3월 Anthropic.
AI를 만든 회사 3곳이 3개월 연속으로
무료 교육을 열었음. 수료증까지 줌.
```

Works when: the reader has already invested money/time in something that's about to be undercut.

### Formula B — Extreme Title + Bigger Bet + Timer
Template: `{과거 극단 숫자}를 {극단 동사}한 "{역사상 가장 X}" 타이틀을 가진 적 있는 {주어}가 이번엔 {새로운 극단 숫자}를 {새 동작}. {시한}이 붙어 있음.`

Source: 215k/200k view post (손정의 OpenAI).
```
105조 원을 잃어서 "역사상 가장 많이 잃은 사람"
타이틀을 가진 적 있는 사람이
이번엔 90조 원을 AI 하나에 걸었음.
12개월짜리 시한이 붙어 있는 상태로.
```

Works when: one protagonist has a track record of extreme wins AND extreme losses and is about to trigger the next chapter.

### Formula C — Unexpected Subject + Extreme Action + Ratio
Template: `{예상 밖 주어} {구체 동작} {구체 숫자}. {비교 기준}. {맥락 한 줄로}.`

Source: 201k view post (켄터키 할머니).
```
켄터키 82세 할머니가 350억원을 거절했음.
시세의 10배.
이름도 안 밝힌 대형 테크 기업이
농장 땅에 데이터센터 짓겠다고 한 건데.
```

Works when: the subject is someone the reader would NEVER expect to be the agent of a major corporate story.

### Formula D — Company + Timestamp + Reaction Scale + Paradigm
Template: `{회사}가 {시점} 공개한 {무엇}. {반응 숫자}. {같은 날 피해자}. {패러다임 선언}. {미끼 한 줄}.`

Source: 109k view post (Google Stitch).
```
Google이 어제 공개한 AI 디자인 툴.
발표 트윗 조회수 700만.
같은 날 Adobe 주가 하락.
말로 디자인하는 시대가 옴.
무료로.
Google이 직접 이름 붙임: "바이브 디자인."
```

Works when: a major player drops a product that creates immediate collateral damage to a named competitor.

### Formula E — Pure Ratio / Scale Contrast
Template: `{작은 시작 숫자}로 시작한 {주체}가 {짧은 기간} 만에 {압도적 결과 숫자}를 찍었음. {본인 규모 숫자}. {경쟁자 대조 숫자}.`

Source: 150k view post (Medvi 1인 5,600억).
```
2,800만 원으로 시작한 1인 회사가 14개월 만에 매출 5,600억 원을 찍었음.
직원은 본인 포함 2명. 경쟁사는 직원 2,400명.
```

Works when: the story lives inside a single extreme ratio (starting capital → revenue, team size → competitor size). The contrast IS the hook. Save the company/person name for slide 2.

Exception to the "2 proper nouns" rule: if the ratio between any two numbers is > 100x, the contrast alone can carry slide 1.

## Fail patterns (from JSup's weaker posts)

### Pattern 1 — Scene description instead of fact density
```
건물 안에 적이 있음.
먼저 들어가는 건 병사가 아님.
소총을 등에 진 로봇 개.
```
Problem: zero numbers, one proper noun, no time marker, poetic rhythm.

### Pattern 2 — Paradox without numbers
```
Apple이 바이브 코딩 앱을 앱스토어에서 막기 시작함.
```
Problem: contradiction is there, but zero numbers = no cognitive dissonance.

### Pattern 3 — Insider jargon in slide 1
```
...내부 보안 등급 Sev 1.
```
Problem: "Sev 1" filters out the non-engineer reader.

### Pattern 4 — Positive news with no reader stake
```
Anthropic이 "Claude Architect" 자격증을 만들었음.
```
Problem: news-neutral + the door is closed to the reader. No personal stake = no share.

## Hook rules (from SOUL.md, canonical home now here)

The hook should do one of these:
- present a contradiction
- withhold the real meaning
- introduce a powerful loser/winner dynamic
- expose a cost shift or hidden consequence
- show a number that changes the perceived scale

The hook should NOT:
- explain the full story
- summarize the conclusion
- sound like a news headline rewrite

### Hook intensity calibration

The hook must be UNDERSTATED. State one disturbing fact plainly. No emphasis words. Let the reader's brain do the emotional work.

Each hook delivers ONE surprising fact and withholds everything else. The reader MUST scroll to find out.

Calibration:
- Too weak: "Perplexity에 대한 소송이 있었음." (no tension)
- Too strong: "Perplexity가 시크릿 모드에서도 대화를 Meta/Google에 3년간 넘겼다. 집단소송." (everything revealed)
- Correct: "Perplexity 쓰는 사람한테 불편한 소식. 시크릿 모드를 켜도 소용없었음." (discomfort created, reason withheld)

## Slide pacing rules (CRITICAL — from SOUL.md, canonical home now here)

### One reveal per slide

Each slide gets ONE new piece of information. Not two, not four. ONE.

If slide 1 creates a question, slide 2 answers THAT question while creating a NEW one. This chain must not break until the close.

### Information budget

| Slide | Role | Reveals | Withholds |
|-------|------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | Hook | WHAT happened (one fact) | HOW, WHY, WHO benefits |
| 2 | Mechanism | HOW it happened | Full scale, emotional impact |
| 3 | Scale/proof | Numbers, evidence | The deeper paradox |
| 4 | Reversal | The twist or emotional turn | Resolution |
| 5 | Escalation | Broader implications | What happens next |
| 6 | Context | Why this matters systemically | Final judgment |
| 7 | Open close | Unresolved tension | Everything — loop stays open |

### Anti-pattern: front-loading

NEVER put the what, how, who, and consequence all in slide 1. This kills scroll motivation.

Bad: "A가 B를 C에게 넘겼음. D년 동안. 소송 제기됨." (4 reveals in 1 slide)
Good: "A 쓰는 사람한테 불편한 소식." (1 reveal: something bad happened to YOU)

### Self-check before delivering

For every slide 1, ask: "If I only read this slide, would I NEED to read slide 2?"
If the answer is no — too much is revealed. Cut until the answer is yes.

## NEW: 한 메시지 원칙 (One Message Rule)

Every thread must deliver ONE core message. Not two, not three.

Test: "이 글의 핵심 메시지를 1문장으로 요약할 수 있는가?"
- YES → proceed
- NO → 메시지가 분산됨. 가장 강한 1개를 골라 나머지 버려라.

Source: 쓰레드마케팅자료 PDF 3-4: "스레드 게시물 한 개 당: 전하고자 하는 말 한가지. 글을 읽고 독자가 한가지를 기억해야지 성공."

## NEW: 반전 포인트 존재 확인 (Reversal Check)

Every 100k+ JSup post has a reversal between slide 3-5:
- 306k: "무료 교육" → reversal: "생태계 전쟁"
- 215k: "역대급 투자" → reversal: "12개월 시한, WeWork 트라우마"
- 201k: "350억 거절" → reversal: "옆 땅은 이미 팔림"
- 150k: "매출 5,600억" → reversal: "IDOR 보안 구멍, 가짜 의사"
- 109k: "무료 디자인 툴" → reversal: "SaaS 420조 증발"

Test: "slide 3~5 사이에 독자의 초기 해석을 뒤집는 반전이 존재하는가?"
- NO → rewrite. Add reversal or abandon topic.

## NEW: 구체적 장면 존재 확인 (Concrete Scene Check)

At least 1 slide must contain a specific scene: time + place + actor + action/dialogue.

Good (150k slide 4): "코드를 수정하고 등산을 갔음. 미디어 에이전시에서 전화가 옴. '1시간째 주문이 0건입니다.' 프로덕션 서버가 죽어 있었음."

Good (201k slide 3): "우리 할아버지, 증조할아버지가 여기서 세금 내고, 나라를 먹여 살렸어."

Bad: "조 단위 현금 흐름이 수년간 끊기지 않아야 함." (abstract, no scene)

## Pre-draft hook checklist

Run this BEFORE writing slide 2. If any box is unchecked, rewrite the hook.

- [ ] Does slide 1 contain at least 3 concrete numbers? (Formula E winners hit 4-5)
- [ ] Does slide 1 contain at least 2 proper nouns — OR a ratio between two numbers that is > 100x?
- [ ] Does slide 1 contain a specific time marker (date / duration / "어제" / "14개월 만에")?
- [ ] Does slide 1 contain an extreme-state phrase or contrast?
- [ ] Does slide 1 either target a reader persona directly OR lead with an overwhelming scale number OR lead with an extreme ratio?
- [ ] Does slide 1 avoid jargon that a non-engineer reader would not recognize?
- [ ] If the reader only read slide 1, would they NEED slide 2 to make sense of it?
- [ ] Is the hook written in 반말, clipped lines, no hype adjectives?
- [ ] Can the thread's core message be summarized in 1 sentence? (한 메시지 원칙)
- [ ] Does a reversal exist between slide 3-5?
- [ ] Does at least 1 slide contain a concrete scene (time+place+actor+dialogue)?

## Core questions (preserved from original)

- Does slide 1 create discomfort, contradiction, or curiosity?
- Does slide 1 avoid explaining the full story?
- If a reader only sees slide 1, do they still need slide 2?
- Is the hook stated plainly rather than with hype words?
- Is the hook targeted enough to make the right reader feel implicated?

## Good pattern (preserved from original)

- one surprising fact
- one missing answer
